אלי נאיר צהרי ענדי דינ’ ואחד (ʾly nʾyr ṣhri ʿndi dyn’ wʾḥd), “I owe my brother-in-law Nāyar one dinar” (TS 20.137, line 2.6). The term ṣihr refers to in-law in general, and may be either father-in-law, brother-in-law or son-in-law. In the case of Aśu, an in-law through matrimonial alliance to her could only be her brother, for nāyars follow a matrilineal system, where the brother is of more significance to the woman’s kinship relations than the father (Gough 1961, 352-4).
